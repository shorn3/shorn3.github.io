More about My Hobbies

  • Piano

    The piano was invented by Bartolomeo Cristofori in Italy around 1700 as an improvement on the harpsichord, which plucked strings, making it hard to control volume. Cristofori's new instrument, called the "pianoforte" (soft-loud), used hammers to strike strings, allowing players to play both quietly (piano) and loudly (forte) by hitting the keys harder or softer. Though not instantly popular, it grew to become the famous instrument we know today, with iron frames and pedals added later for even more powerful sound and control, becoming central to classical and modern music.(Wikipedia)

  • Painting

    Painting's history spans from prehistoric cave art (40,000+ years ago) using natural pigments to depict hunts, evolving through ancient civilizations (Egyptians, Greeks, Romans) with religious and daily life scenes, the Middle Ages' icons, the Renaissance's realism, and modern/contemporary abstraction, with ongoing innovation in techniques, materials (oil, acrylic, digital), and purpose, moving from ritual to self-expression and market appeal.(Wikipedia)

  • Swimming

    Swimming is a, low-impact, full-body aquatic activity, widely recognized for both recreational enjoyment and as a highly competitive sport. It involves moving through water using coordinated arm and leg motions, providing significant cardiovascular, muscular, and mental health benefits.(Brittanica)
