Extra info

Belgium

Belgium is a small, densely populated Western European federal state known for its complex governance, cultural diversity, and role as a European hub, with Dutch-speaking Flanders in the north and French-speaking Wallonia in the south, plus the bilingual capital, Brussels. It's a constitutional monarchy with a strong economy, famous for its rich history, medieval towns, and as the de facto home of the EU institutions.

Morocco

Morocco, in northwest Africa, is a kingdom known for its unique blend of Arab, Berber, European, and African cultures, located where the Atlantic meets the Mediterranean, near Spain. It features diverse geography, from the Atlas Mountains to the Sahara Desert, with a stable constitutional monarchy led by a King, and boasts vibrant cities, bustling souks (markets), and rich history as a former French protectorate. Islam is the state religion, Arabic and Berber are official languages, and its capital is Rabat, with Casablanca being the largest city.

Finland

Finland is a Nordic country in Northern Europe known for its vast forests, thousands of lakes, high quality of life, strong welfare system, and status as one of the world's happiest nations, with its capital in Helsinki, official languages Finnish and Swedish, and a history as a former part of Sweden and Russia before gaining independence in 1917. It has by clean nature, high technology, and a culture valuing honesty, privacy, and outdoor activities like saunas and sports.
