Pizelle Recipe

Pizelle recipe and background

History

Pizelles are considered the oldest known cookie. They originated in the mid section of Italy.
They were originally made for a celebration called the "Festival of the Snakes". It was a celebration in the village of Colcullo in the region of Abruzzo.
It was once overrun by snakes but they were finally chased out. Every year they celebrate that day with the festival and make Pizelles for the occasion.
Pizelles are round and flat and they got their name from the Italian word for that which is "pizze". They normally have waffle designs or other fun designs on them,
in some parts of Italy they would even be made with the family crest and that would be passed down.

The recipe

To make pizzelle batter,you combine melted butter with eggs, sugar, flour, baking powder, and the flavoring of your choosing.
The dough browns more evenly if it has some time to sit, so it's reccomended to make the dough in advance and store it in the fridge until it's ready.
To cook the batter, you’ll need a pizzelle press.

Ingredients

  • 8 tablespoons (1 stick) unsalted butter
  • 3 large eggs
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • Cooking spray
  • Powdered sugar, for serving (optional)
Directions
  1. Cut 8 tablespoons unsalted butter into pieces. Place in a microwave-safe bowl and microwave on high until melted, about 45 seconds. Set aside to cool while you measure and mix the rest of the batter.

  2. Place 3 large eggs, 3/4 cup granulated sugar, and the vanilla in a large bowl and whisk until fully mixed. Add 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder, and 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt, and whisk to combine. Add the cooled butter and whisk until completely smooth, about 1 minute. Cover the bowl and let the batter rest for 30 minutes.

  3. About 10 minutes before baking, preheat a pizzelle press. Lightly coat the press with cooking spray and add 2 teaspoons of the batter into the center for each pizzelle. Close the press and cook until lightly golden-brown, 1 to 1 1/2 minutes. Use a thin flat spatula to transfer the cookies to a cooling rack. The cookies will crisp up when cooled.

  4. Repeat with the remaining batter, but you won’t need to coat the press with cooking spray each time, especially if you have a non-stick press. Cool the cookies completely. Dust with powdered sugar before serving if desired
